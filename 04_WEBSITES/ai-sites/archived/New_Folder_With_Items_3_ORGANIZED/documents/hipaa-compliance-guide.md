## üè• What is HIPAA?

### **Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)**
- **Purpose:** Protect patient health information (PHI)
- **Scope:** Healthcare providers, business associates, and covered entities
- **Penalties:** $100 - $1.5 million per violation
- **Compliance:** Required for any business handling PHI

### **Key Terms:**
- **PHI (Protected Health Information):** Any health information that can identify a patient
- **Covered Entity:** Healthcare providers, health plans, healthcare clearinghouses
- **Business Associate:** Any vendor/service provider handling PHI
- **BA Agreement:** Legal contract between covered entity and business associate

## üíº Business Associate Agreements (BAA)

### **What is a BAA?**
- **Legal contract** between you and healthcare clients
- **Defines responsibilities** for PHI protection
- **Required by law** when handling PHI
- **Protects both parties** legally

### **BAA Requirements:**
- **Permitted uses** of PHI
- **Safeguards** you must implement
- **Breach notification** procedures
- **Return/destruction** of PHI
- **Subcontractor** requirements

### **Sample BAA Clauses:**
```
1. Business Associate agrees to use PHI only for purposes authorized by Covered Entity
2. Business Associate will implement appropriate safeguards to protect PHI
3. Business Associate will report any security incidents involving PHI
4. Business Associate will return or destroy PHI upon termination of agreement
5. Business Associate will ensure subcontractors also comply with HIPAA
```
