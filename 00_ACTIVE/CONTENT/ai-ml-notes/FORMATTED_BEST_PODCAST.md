## üîÑ The Tolerance-Dominance Cycle: A Historical Framework

### Phase 1: Strategic Moderation
Groups rebrand as "protectors of heritage" or "defenders of free speech," hosting community events or dialogues to sanitize their image. This phase aims to:
- Recruit disillusioned moderates
- Deflect accusations of bigotry
- Build mainstream credibility
- Prepare for more aggressive tactics

### Phase 2: The Shift to Dominance
When strategic moderation fails to achieve desired outcomes, groups pivot to more aggressive tactics:
- Open hostility toward perceived enemies
- Direct confrontation with opposing groups
- Escalation of rhetoric and actions
- Mobilization of base supporters

### Phase 3: The Cycle Repeats
After dominance tactics face resistance or backlash, groups return to moderation:
- Rebranding efforts
- "Unity" initiatives
- Attempts to appear reasonable
- Preparation for next cycle
