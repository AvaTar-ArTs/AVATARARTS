<strong>The Curious Case of Mr. Thompson's Watch</strong>
In the heart of London, there lived a man named Mr. Thompson. He was an ordinary man with an ordinary job, living an ordinary life. Every day, he would wake up at 6:30 am, put on his grey suit, and walk to the train station to commute to his office. His life was a series of routines, and he liked it that way. But there was one thing that set Mr. Thompson apart from everyone else: his pocket watch.
This wasn't just any pocket watch. It was a family heirloom, passed down from generation to generation. The watch was said to have a peculiar power: it could predict the exact time of its owner's death. However, Mr. Thompson never believed in such tales and considered it a mere family myth.
One day, as Mr. Thompson was cleaning the watch, he noticed an inscription on the back that he had never seen before. It read, "12:45." Curious, he thought about the family legend and chuckled, thinking it was a mere coincidence.
The next day, as he was walking to the train station, he decided to take a different route, just for a change. As he was crossing the street, he heard the distant chime of a clock tower. He pulled out his pocket watch to check the time. It was exactly 12:45 pm.
Suddenly, a gust of wind blew his hat off his head. Instinctively, Mr. Thompson bent down to pick it up, narrowly missing a speeding car that would've hit him had he not bent down. He was shaken but unharmed. He looked at his watch again, and to his astonishment, the hands were stuck at 12:45.
Relieved, he continued his day, thinking he had cheated death. However, as he reached his office, he realized he had forgotten an important file at home. Deciding to head back, he waited for the elevator. As the doors opened, he stepped in without looking, not realizing the elevator was out of service and there was no cabin. He fell down the shaft.
The office clock read 12:45 pm.
The irony was not lost on those who knew the legend of Mr. Thompson's watch. While he had escaped one tragic fate, he met another, all at the very time his watch had predicted. The watch, it seemed, had the last laugh.
