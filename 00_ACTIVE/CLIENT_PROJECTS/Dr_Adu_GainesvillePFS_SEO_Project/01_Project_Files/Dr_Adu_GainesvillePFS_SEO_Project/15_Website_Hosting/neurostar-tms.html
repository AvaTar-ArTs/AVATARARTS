What is NeuroStar TMS Therapy?

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) is a non-invasive, FDA-approved treatment for depression that uses magnetic fields to stimulate nerve cells in the brain. Unlike medication, TMS therapy doesn't cause systemic side effects and can be effective for patients who haven't responded to traditional treatments.

How TMS Therapy Works

During TMS treatment, an electromagnetic coil is placed against your scalp near your forehead. The coil delivers magnetic pulses that stimulate nerve cells in the region of your brain involved in mood control and depression. These magnetic pulses are similar to those used in MRI machines and are generally well-tolerated.

Why Choose TMS Over Medication?

  • No Systemic Side Effects: Unlike antidepressants, TMS doesn't cause weight gain, sexual dysfunction, or other common medication side effects
  • Non-Invasive: No surgery, anesthesia, or recovery time required
  • Targeted Treatment: Directly stimulates the specific brain regions involved in depression
  • Proven Effectiveness: Clinical studies show significant improvement in depression symptoms
  • Maintains Daily Activities: You can drive yourself to and from appointments

Frequently Asked Questions About TMS Therapy

How long does each TMS session take?

Each TMS session typically lasts 20-40 minutes. You'll be awake and alert throughout the treatment, and you can read, watch TV, or simply relax.

How many TMS sessions will I need?

Most patients receive 5 sessions per week for 4-6 weeks, for a total of 20-30 sessions. The exact number depends on your individual response to treatment.

What does TMS therapy feel like?

Most patients describe TMS as a tapping or knocking sensation on the scalp. Some may experience mild discomfort initially, but this typically decreases over time.

When will I start to see results?

Some patients begin to notice improvement within 2-4 weeks, while others may take 4-6 weeks. Response varies from person to person.

Is TMS therapy covered by insurance?

Most insurance plans cover TMS therapy for treatment-resistant depression. We'll help verify your coverage and handle the insurance process.

Can I continue taking my current medications?

Yes, TMS therapy can be used alongside your current medications. Dr. Adu will work with you to optimize your treatment plan.

Are there any side effects of TMS therapy?

The most common side effect is mild scalp discomfort during treatment. Some patients may experience mild headache or facial muscle twitching, which typically resolve quickly.

How long do the results last?

Many patients experience long-lasting improvement in depression symptoms. Some may need occasional maintenance sessions, while others maintain their improvement without additional treatment.
