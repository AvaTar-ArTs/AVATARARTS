What is Forensic Psychiatry?

Forensic psychiatry is a specialized branch of psychiatry that focuses on the intersection of mental health and the legal system. Dr. Lawrence Adu provides expert forensic psychiatric services to attorneys, courts, and legal professionals throughout Florida.

Forensic psychiatrists evaluate individuals involved in legal proceedings to assess their mental state, competency, and provide expert opinions that assist the court in making informed decisions.

Forensic Services We Provide

Competency Evaluations

Comprehensive evaluations to assess an individual's competency to stand trial, make legal decisions, or manage their affairs. These evaluations help determine if a person has the mental capacity to understand legal proceedings and assist in their defense.

  • Criminal competency evaluations
  • Civil competency assessments
  • Guardianship evaluations
  • Capacity assessments

Expert Witness Testimony

Dr. Adu provides expert witness testimony in court proceedings, offering professional psychiatric opinions based on comprehensive evaluations and evidence-based assessments.

  • Court testimony
  • Deposition testimony
  • Expert reports
  • Case consultations

Forensic Psychiatric Evaluations

Comprehensive psychiatric evaluations for legal cases, including assessments of mental state, risk factors, and treatment recommendations.

  • Criminal responsibility evaluations
  • Mental state at time of offense
  • Risk assessments
  • Treatment recommendations

Legal Case Consultations

Consultation services for attorneys and legal professionals on psychiatric aspects of cases, helping to develop legal strategies and understand mental health factors.

  • Case review and analysis
  • Psychiatric case strategy
  • Document review
  • Expert opinion development

Forensic Evaluation Process

  1. Initial Consultation: Review of case details and determination of evaluation needs
  2. Comprehensive Evaluation: Thorough psychiatric assessment including clinical interview, review of records, and psychological testing if indicated
  3. Report Preparation: Detailed written report with findings, opinions, and recommendations
  4. Expert Testimony: Court testimony or deposition as needed to present findings and opinions
